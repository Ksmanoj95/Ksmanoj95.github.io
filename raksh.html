Manoj Kumar

RAKSH

Non-Invasive Asthma & Pneumonia monitoring device

One of the greatest problems faced by NGO healthcare organizations and Government Hospitals in remote areas in developing countries is the shortage of doctors and medical personnel. Respiratory ailments, especially pneumonia is a serious threat to children in rural areas and has been the single major cause of death for children under age of 5.

As pneumonia has a relatively long recovery time, most parents who are daily wage workers cannot afford expensive hospital care and forgo treatment altogether due to frequent tests and scans required. Current solutions are developed for in-hospital care and tend to be expensive as they are designed from the context of developed nations.

Raksh non-invasively tracks a child’s respiration rate, body temperature and also the relative humidity of the child’s breath. The data from the device is streamed to either a BLE compatible smartphone or a smart Bluetooth Internet gateway device, which uploads the data to a server.

The patient's information is presented to a doctor via a web page or an app using intelligent graphs and analytics highlighting abnormal conditions such as instances of abnormal breathing or high body temperature. The device also enables doctors to track if medication is effective by seeing changes in the various physiological parameters.

This will enable health workers in rural areas that are extremely understaffed to assist more children. The device would also have alarm modes to alert parents to sudden onset of fever or difficulty breathing.
